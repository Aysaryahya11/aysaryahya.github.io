I am **Aysar Yahya**, a Ph.D. student in **Communication and Information with a concentration in Advertising at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville**, and a Ph.D. candidate in **Public Relations and Advertising at Anadolu University, Türkiye**.

My research focuses on **digital advertising, consumer privacy, data surveillance, and responsible use of emerging technologies in communication**. I am particularly interested in understanding how individuals perceive personalized advertising, privacy disclosures, algorithmic systems, and artificial intelligence in digital environments.

My current research explores the intersection of **privacy, advertising effectiveness, and computational social science**, with an emphasis on topics such as targeted advertising, cookie consent, privacy paradox, and ethical challenges in AI-driven advertising.

I have published research articles in the field of advertising and privacy, and my work has appeared in journals such as the *Journal of Marketing Communications* and the *International Journal of Advertising*. I also participated in the **Summer Institute in Computational Social Science (SICSS) at Carnegie Mellon University**, where I received training in computational methods, machine learning, text analysis, network analysis, and digital trace data research.

Before joining the University of Tennessee, I completed my **M.A. in Public Relations and Advertising at Anadolu University**, where my thesis examined the effect of advertising disclosure on influencer avoidance. My academic journey has been shaped by an interest in how communication technologies influence consumer behavior and society.

Outside research, I am interested in teaching advertising, digital and social media communication, consumer behavior, advertising ethics, and research methods.

**Research Interests**

* Digital Privacy and the Privacy Paradox
* Targeted Advertising and Consumer Behavior
* Responsible AI in Advertising
* Computational Social Science Applications in Communication
* Digital Advertising Ethics
